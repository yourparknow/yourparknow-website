3. The Legal Nature of Your Use — License, Not Bailment

This section is critical. Please read it carefully.

3.1 You receive a License, not a Bailment

When you book a parking space through YourParkNow, what you receive is a LICENSE — a contractual permission to use the space for a defined period. You do NOT enter into a bailment relationship with the Host, with YourParkNow, or with any other party.

A bailment requires that you hand over possession and control of your vehicle to another party (such as a valet who takes your keys). In YourParkNow, this never happens. You:

  • Drive your own vehicle to the space.
  • Park your own vehicle without assistance from any attendant.
  • Keep your own keys at all times.
  • Lock your own vehicle.
  • Retrieve your own vehicle when you leave.

Because no possession or control of your vehicle is ever transferred to any other party, no bailment is created under Florida law. This is the same legal structure that applies when you park at a street meter, in a non-attended public lot, or in your own driveway.

3.2 Consequences of License vs. Bailment

Because the relationship is a License, the following is true:

  • No party (not YourParkNow, not the Host, not anyone) takes on a duty to safeguard your vehicle.
  • No party is responsible for theft, vandalism, damage, scratches, dents, weather damage, or any other harm to your vehicle.
  • No party is responsible for items left inside your vehicle.
  • Your own automobile insurance is the only source of protection for your vehicle.
